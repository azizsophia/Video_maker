## YouTube description (no emojis)
Surah Al-Kahf (chapter 18) contains four stories that look unrelated: the People of the Cave, the Two Gardens, Musa and al-Khidr, and Dhul-Qarnayn. Scholars have long noted they share one thread: each is a trial of what people value most, faith, wealth, knowledge, and power. The Qur'an states the world itself is a test (18:7). The Prophet taught that the first ten verses protect from the Dajjal (Sahih Muslim), and that reading it on Friday brings light between the two Fridays (authenticated by al-Albani).

Sources:
- Surah Al-Kahf (18), Meccan, 110 verses
- The four stories: 18:9-26, 18:32-44, 18:60-82, 18:83-98
- Quran 18:7 (the world as a test)
- Sahih Muslim 809; the Friday recitation hadith (Sahih al-Jami 6470)
