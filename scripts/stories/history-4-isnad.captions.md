## YouTube description (no emojis)
To preserve the Prophet Muhammad's exact words, Muslims developed the isnad: a documented chain of narrators for every single hadith. They built an entire science of narrator criticism (ilm al-rijal), recording the honesty, memory, and life dates of hundreds of thousands of transmitters. Imam al-Bukhari examined around 600,000 narrations and accepted only about 7,000 into his Sahih. Every hadith is graded by its chain: authentic, good, weak, or fabricated.
