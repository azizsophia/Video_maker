## YouTube description (no emojis)
Around 570 CE, Abraha, the Aksumite viceroy of Yemen, marched on Mecca with war elephants to destroy the Ka'ba and divert pilgrimage to a cathedral he had built. Tradition records that the lead elephant refused to advance on the Ka'ba, and the army was then destroyed by flocks of birds dropping stones of baked clay, as told in Surah Al-Fil. By tradition, this was the same year the Prophet Muhammad was born.

Sources:
- Surah Al-Fil (105)
- Sira of Ibn Ishaq: Abraha's march, the elephant's refusal, the destruction of the army
- Traditional dating of the Year of the Elephant (c. 570 CE)
