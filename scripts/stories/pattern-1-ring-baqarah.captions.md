## YouTube description
For two centuries, Western critics dismissed the Quran as disorganized. Then modern structural analysis revealed something stunning: Surah Al-Baqarah, the Quran's longest chapter, is built as a concentric ring. The opening mirrors the ending, law mirrors law, story mirrors story, all folding inward to a single center: the command to face the Ka'ba in prayer. The structure itself carries the meaning.

What makes it impossible: the Quran was revealed in fragments over years, in response to live events, by an unlettered man who could not read or write, with the Prophet directing where each verse was placed, no drafts and no edits, yet it forms a flawless mirror that scholars only mapped recently.
