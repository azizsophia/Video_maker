# Ketabi Studio — Content & Growth Strategy

> **Prime directive (overrides everything below):** every video must be
> **Islamically respectful and 100% accurate, with a source for every claim.**
> Ajr first, reach second, business third — in that order. If a "great hook"
> requires shading the truth, the hook is wrong, not the truth.

This doc turns our real performance data into a repeatable system: what to make,
why it works, how to stay copyright-safe, and how it feeds the business.

---

## 1. The business this content serves

Ketabi Studio (`ketabistudio.com`) is a full **Islamic gift house for all ages** —
personalized storybooks, photo keepsake books, and vetted-dua greeting cards
(Eid, weddings, new babies), plus the Ketabi app. The shop is pre-launch; the
live ask is **"Join the founding list."**

So the channel is a **funnel**, not the product:

```
ajr-earning video  →  viewer trusts the brand  →  ketabistudio.com
                                                   → join founding list / app
                                                   → buys when the shop opens
```

**CTA rules (adab-first):**
- Never put a product/sales CTA over a Qur'an ayah or in the spiritual climax.
- Brand + "join the founding list at ketabistudio.com" goes at the **very end**,
  after the content has landed, and in the **bio + pinned comment**.
- The video must be worth watching even if you never click. That's what earns
  both the ajr and the trust that sells.

2. **Prophecy + "the Qur'an said this 1,400 years ago"** is the single strongest vein.

   cliffhanger ("Part 2 tomorrow").
5. **Pure recitation underperforms** — and it's also the copyright liability. The
   data and the legal risk point the same way: demote standalone recitation.

## 3. Content pillars (all map to proven winners)

| Pillar | Hook engine | Example seeds | Source discipline |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Prophecy / "they said it 1400 years ago"** | foretelling + today | Euphrates & the gold; smoke; the barefoot bedouins building towers | Qur'an + *sahih* hadith only; never claim a sign is *fulfilled* unless it is |
| **Archaeology / real places** | "scientists found…" | Pharaoh's body; Iram of the Pillars; Mada'in Salih | cite the ayah/hadith **and** the documented find separately; never conflate |
| **The afterlife journey** | what happens next | grave, gathering, the scale, the bridge | Qur'an + *sahih* hadith; named graders |
| **Heroes (Prophets & Sahaba)** | character + stakes | Khalid, Abu Bakr, Yusuf, Maryam | Seerah from reliable sources; **never depict faces** |
| **"Decoded" (a surah's secret)** | structure/meaning | the ring of Al-Baqarah, Ar-Rahman's refrain | mainstream tafsir (Ibn Kathir, etc.), attributed |

**Diversify the funnel:** prophecy/archaeology bring the broad all-ages reach;
afterlife and "decoded" build the iman + trust that converts to the brand.

## 4. Production: how we stand out (Remotion + real footage)

The reason most Islamic short-form looks identical is shared canned templates.
Our moat is a **programmable engine** + a **consistent brand look** nobody can
copy in a tap.

- **Signature layer (Remotion):** animated Arabic typography, the "noor" brand
  palette (cream / ink-green / gold), tajweed coloring, hifz word-blanking,
  word-by-word sync, the citation chips. This is the ownable part.
- **Scene layer (real footage):** licensed archaeology / landscape / cosmic
  B-roll (Pexels, Pixabay Video, and other commercial-clear libraries) behind
  the story beats — the look that's already winning (AlUla, deserts, ancient
  walls).
- **Audio:** AI English narration (ElevenLabs "Daniel") for the *English only*.
  Ambient sound effects (wind, rumble) — **never melodic/instrumental music.**

### Non-negotiable visual guardrails (Islamic adab)
- **No depiction of the Prophet ﷺ, any Prophet, or the Sahaba's faces** — ever,
  including AI-generated faces. Use landscapes, real archaeological footage,
  manuscripts, calligraphy, light/noor, silhouettes, abstract motion.
- The **Qur'an is never AI-voiced.** Only a real reciter (see §5).
- No music/instruments anywhere.

## 5. The copyright fix (recitation audio)

Decision: **verified free-license reciter** for the short ayah moments.

**Verified facts (primary sources):**
- EveryAyah / VerseByVerseQuran audio + timings = **CC-BY-NC** → *non-commercial
  only* → **not usable** on a monetized channel.
- Wikimedia Commons hosts recitations under CC0 / CC-BY-SA / Public Domain Mark,
  but **per file** — verify each individually.
- Internet Archive has full-mushaf sets tagged Public Domain by uploaders (e.g.
  Saad Al-Ghamdi) — a real lead, but an uploader tag isn't proof against a false
  Content ID claim (disputable, not a strike).
- Qur'anic **text** = public domain; a reciter's **performance** = separately
  copyrighted.

**Operating rules:**
1. Keep recited audio to **short quoted ayahs (≈3–8s)** inside stories, where
   block risk is lowest — which the data already wants us to do.
2. Source those clips from a **verified CC0/PD recording** (shortlist below,
   pending your approval). Log the license + URL for every clip used.
3. **Fallback that's 100% safe:** record our own recitation of the short ayah.
4. For any full word-by-word video later: use a free-license recitation +
   **forced alignment** to generate our own timings (see `docs/SETUP.md`).

**To verify next (then you approve):** specific Wikimedia/Archive recordings by
surah, with license + URL, so we have a known-clean library for the ayahs our
scripts use.

## 6. Publishing (see also `docs/PUBLISHING.md`)

- **Both platforms equally**, per current goal.
- **YouTube Shorts:** ≤60s cut is always copyright-safe; test any >60s cut
  Unlisted first.
- **TikTok:** post the 60s+ cut (TikTok monetization needs 60s+).
- **Serialize** multi-part stories; end Part 1 on a cliffhanger.
- Reciter credited in every description; sources listed in the description too —
  it builds trust and is good adab.

### Cadence (sustainable, engine-driven)
- 3–4 shorts/week, batched. Lead with prophecy/archaeology; rotate in
  afterlife/decoded for depth.
- Every short ends: spiritual beat → brand card → "founding list at
  ketabistudio.com" (bio + pinned comment carry the link).

## 7. Hook bank (first-2-seconds openers, all must be truthful)

- "Fourteen hundred years ago, a man who couldn't read described something
  we're watching happen right now."
- "There's a place in the Qur'an scientists only confirmed last century."
- "A drowning tyrant. A 3,000-year-old body. And one ayah that called it."
- "The Prophet ﷺ named the sign — and told us *not* to take from it."
- "This isn't folklore. It's in the Qur'an, with chapter and verse."

Each must be backed by §3's source discipline. A hook that overstates ("they
FOUND the gold!") is a lie — reframe to what's actually true ("the river is
drying, and the prophecy is about what happens when it does").
