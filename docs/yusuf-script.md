# The Story of Yusuf (alayhi salam) — long-form script + shot list

Draft for sign-off. Storytelling first, every claim sourced on screen, authentic
only (Qur'an by verse; hadith from Bukhari/Muslim; the Makkah parallel from
seerah). No Israiliyyat, no weak narrations. Honorifics included.

Each beat: **[SHOT]** = the literal footage on screen (one distinct clip per
beat, never reused) · narration (written with pauses for an emotional read) ·
*(source shown in the corner)*.

## Act I — The dream, and the betrayal

1. **[SHOT: a single oil lamp flickering in the dark]**
   "In the whole of the Qur'an, there is only one story told from beginning to end, in a single chapter. And Allah Himself calls it the most beautiful of them all. This is the story of Yusuf, alayhi salam." *(Qur'an 12:3)*

2. **[SHOT: a night sky, eleven bright stars, the moon]** + on-screen ayah
   "It begins with a boy, and a dream. One night, young Yusuf sees eleven stars, and the sun, and the moon, all bowing down before him. He runs to his father, the Prophet Yaqub, alayhi salam." *(Qur'an 12:4, shown not recited)*

3. **[SHOT: an old man's hand resting gently on a small child's shoulder]**
   "His father's face changes. He knows what this means. This child has been chosen. So he warns him, softly: tell no one of this dream. Above all, not your brothers." *(Qur'an 12:5)*

4. **[SHOT: a group of grown men, in shadow, turning away coldly]**
   "Because his brothers already cannot stand him. They watch their father's love settle on this boy, and it eats at them, until they decide to be rid of him for good." *(Qur'an 12:8-9)*

5. **[SHOT: looking up from the bottom of a dark stone well to a small circle of daylight]**
   "They take him out to play. And they throw him down into the black of a deep well. A child, alone at the bottom of a pit, listening to his brothers' footsteps walk away." *(Qur'an 12:15)*

6. **[SHOT: a lone wolf moving through dim woodland]** + on-screen ayah
   "That night they come home weeping. A wolf, they say, devoured Yusuf. And they hold out his shirt, soaked in blood. But Yaqub knows his sons are lying. And his heart breaks in a way only a parent could understand." *(Qur'an 12:16-18, shown not recited)*

7. **[SHOT: a worn caravan of camels crossing dunes at dusk]**
   "Days later, a caravan stops at the well and lowers a bucket. And up comes a boy. They carry him to Egypt, and sell him there, for a few cheap coins, as if he were worth nothing." *(Qur'an 12:19-20)*

8. **[SHOT: the animated Canaan -> Egypt map]**
   "Hundreds of miles from his father, from his home, from everyone who loved him. Sold into a strange land." *(Qur'an 12:21)*

## Act II — Egypt, the test, the prison

9. **[SHOT: towering ancient Egyptian columns / a grand stone hall]**
   "But Allah had placed him in the house of one of the most powerful men in Egypt, a minister, who told his wife: take good care of him. And there, the boy grew into a young man." *(Qur'an 12:21-22)*

10. **[SHOT: radiant light breaking across a dark sky (the night journey)]**
    "And not an ordinary young man. The Prophet, peace be upon him, told us that on the night he was raised through the heavens, he met Yusuf, and that Yusuf had been given half of all the beauty in creation." *(Sahih Muslim 162)*

11. **[SHOT: an ornate old door being bolted shut by lamplight]**
    "Then came the test that would have broken most men. The minister's wife bolted every door in the house, and turned to him." *(Qur'an 12:23)*

12. **[SHOT: a hand turning away from temptation toward a window of light]**
    "And Yusuf refused. He said: I seek refuge in Allah. Your husband has honoured me and trusted me. How could I ever betray him? How could I disobey my Lord?" *(Qur'an 12:23)*

13. **[SHOT: a fleeing figure's robe torn from behind, fabric ripping]**
    "He ran for the door. She caught his shirt from behind, and tore it. And there, at the door, they were seen." *(Qur'an 12:25)*

14. **[SHOT: women at a feast holding fruit and small knives (hands, no faces)]**
    "The women of the city whispered about her. So she gathered them at a feast, gave them fruit and knives, and called Yusuf in. They were so overcome by him that they cut their own hands without even feeling it." *(Qur'an 12:31)*

15. **[SHOT: heavy iron prison bars, a narrow stone cell]**
    "She threatened him: obey me, or be thrown in prison. And Yusuf, alayhi salam, made his choice. My Lord, he prayed, prison is dearer to me than the sin they are calling me to. And so, an innocent man, he was locked away." *(Qur'an 12:32-35)*

16. **[SHOT: two young men sitting in the gloom of a cell (silhouettes)]**
    "In that prison, two young men came to him, each shaken by a dream they could not understand. But before Yusuf read a single one, he turned to them with a question." *(Qur'an 12:36-37)*

17. **[SHOT: a single shaft of light falling through a barred window]** + on-screen ayah
    "O my two companions of prison: are many scattered lords better, or Allah, the One, the Supreme?" *(Qur'an 12:39, shown not recited)*

18. **[SHOT: an ornate goblet of wine being poured]**
    "Then he told them the truth of their dreams. One of you will be freed, and pour wine for the king again. The other will not live. And to the one who would be saved, he said: remember me to your master." *(Qur'an 12:41-42)*

19. **[SHOT: an empty cell, a slow beam of light crossing the wall over time]**
    "But the man forgot. And Yusuf remained in that prison, forgotten by the world, for years." *(Qur'an 12:42)*

## Act III — The rise, the famine, the reunion

20. **[SHOT: cattle in a field at dawn, then dry and green ears of wheat]**
    "Until one night, the king of Egypt woke in terror from a dream none of his court could explain. Seven fat cows, devoured by seven starving ones. Seven green ears of grain, and seven withered." *(Qur'an 12:43)*

21. **[SHOT: golden wheat fields, then full granary sacks]**
    "And the freed servant finally remembered the young man in the prison. Yusuf read it for them. Seven years of plenty are coming, then seven years of famine. So plant, and store everything you can. It will save you." *(Qur'an 12:46-49)*

22. **[SHOT: a heavy prison door swinging open to daylight]**
    "The king called for him at once. But Yusuf would not simply walk free. First, he said, clear my name. Ask the women what truly happened. He would leave as an innocent man, or he would not leave at all." *(Qur'an 12:50-51)*

23. **[SHOT: sunlight breaking through dramatic clouds]**
    "The Prophet, peace be upon him, later said that he himself, in Yusuf's place, would have hurried out of that prison at once. But Yusuf waited, for his honour." *(Sahih al-Bukhari 3372)*

24. **[SHOT: vast granaries, sacks and mounds of grain]**
    "His innocence was proven. And the boy who was thrown into a well, and sold, and forgotten in a cell, was placed in charge of the storehouses of all of Egypt." *(Qur'an 12:54-55)*

25. **[SHOT: cracked, dry, lifeless earth]**
    "Then the seven years of famine came. And it spread far beyond Egypt, all the way back to the hills of Canaan, and to ten hungry brothers." *(Qur'an 12:58)*

26. **[SHOT: weary travellers bowing before a seated official (silhouettes)]**
    "They came to Egypt, begging for grain. They bowed before the most powerful man in the land. And they had no idea they were looking at the little brother they had left for dead." *(Qur'an 12:58)*

27. **[SHOT: a golden cup slipped into a sack of grain]**
    "Yusuf tested them. And to keep his youngest brother at his side, he hid the king's own cup in the boy's bag, a plan that Allah taught him." *(Qur'an 12:70, 12:76)*

28. **[SHOT: an old man's weathered, clouded eyes; trembling hands raised]** + on-screen ayah
    "Back home, Yaqub had wept for Yusuf so long that his eyes had turned white with grief. And still, he did not lose hope. I only complain, he said, of my grief and my sorrow, to Allah." *(Qur'an 12:84, 12:86 shown not recited)*

29. **[SHOT: a circle of men frozen in shock, faces in shadow]**
    "When the brothers stood before him one last time, broken and desperate, the moment finally came. Yusuf looked at them, and said: I am Yusuf. Do you know me now?" *(Qur'an 12:89-90)*

30. **[SHOT: open, outstretched hands; warm light]** + on-screen ayah
    "There is no blame upon you today. May Allah forgive you. He is the most merciful of the merciful." *(Qur'an 12:92, shown not recited)*

31. **[SHOT: the gates of an old city, a great crowd gathered]**
    "And centuries later, the Prophet, peace be upon him, would stand over the people of Makkah who had driven him out and tortured his companions, and echo these very words: Go. You are free." *(Conquest of Makkah, seerah)*

32. **[SHOT: a white shirt carried on the wind across open land; then closed eyes opening to light]**
    "Yusuf sent his shirt home. And before it had even arrived, the old father lifted his head: I can sense the scent of Yusuf, though you will think I am a foolish old man. They laid the shirt over his face, and his sight returned." *(Qur'an 12:93-96)*

33. **[SHOT: eleven stars, the sun and the moon over a horizon at dawn]**
    "His parents and his eleven brothers gathered before him and bowed in honour. And the dream of the little boy, the eleven stars, the sun and the moon, had come true, exactly as he had seen it." *(Qur'an 12:100)*

34. **[SHOT: hands raised in dua at first light]** + on-screen ayah
    "My Lord, You gave me authority, and taught me. You are my protector in this world and the next. Let me die in submission to You, and join me with the righteous." *(Qur'an 12:101, shown not recited)*

35. **[SHOT: a single set of footprints on a path leading toward the sunrise]**
    "He was betrayed by his own blood, enslaved, lied about, and left in a prison for years. And every single step was quietly carrying him exactly where Allah meant him to be. So whatever you are walking through, trust the One who is writing your story." *(reflection)*
