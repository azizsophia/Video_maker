# The Story of Yusuf (alayhi salam) — long-form script + shot list (v2)

Draft for sign-off. Story-first (cause and effect, not a list), every claim
sourced on screen, authentic only (Qur'an by verse; hadith from Bukhari/Muslim;
the Makkah parallel from seerah). No Israiliyyat, no weak narrations. Honorifics
included. NO em or en dashes anywhere. Arabic is voiced from a phonetic script
(see appendix) while the on-screen text keeps the proper spelling.

Each beat: **[SHOT]** = the literal footage (one distinct clip per beat, never
reused) and the narration, written with pauses for an emotional read.

## Title sequence

1. **[SHOT: a single oil lamp trembling in the dark, then a premium gold-on-black TITLE CARD rises: "THE STORY OF YUSUF", with a gold flourish, like a film open]**
   "Of all the stories in the Qur'an, only one is told from beginning to end, in a single chapter. And Allah Himself calls it the most beautiful of them all. This is the story of Yusuf, alayhi salam." *(Qur'an 12:3)*

## Act I — The dream, and the betrayal

2. **[SHOT: a vast night sky, eleven bright stars and the moon]** + on-screen ayah
   "It begins with a boy, and a dream. One night, young Yusuf sees eleven stars, and the sun, and the moon, all bowing down before him. Frightened and amazed, he runs to his father, the Prophet Yaqub, alayhi salam." *(Qur'an 12:4, shown not recited)*

3. **[SHOT: an old father's hand closing gently over a small child's hand]**
   "His father's face softens, then turns serious. He knows what a dream like this means. This boy has been chosen. So he holds him close, and warns him, gently. Keep this between us. Do not breathe a word of it to your brothers." *(Qur'an 12:5)*

4. **[SHOT: grown men working a field, glancing back with cold eyes]**
   "Because his brothers were already jealous. They were grown men, strong and many, and still their father's heart leaned toward this one boy. They could not forgive him for it. And quietly, that jealousy hardened into a plan." *(Qur'an 12:8-9)*

5. **[SHOT: the rim of a stone well, then darkness falling away below]**
   "They begged their father to let Yusuf come and play with them. And out in the open, far from home, they threw him down into the darkness of a deep well. Then they stood at the top, and listened to a child cry out, and they turned and walked away." *(Qur'an 12:15)*

6. **[SHOT: a lone wolf standing still in dim woodland]** + on-screen ayah
   "That night they came home weeping, with a story ready. A wolf had taken Yusuf while they raced ahead. And they held up his shirt, stained with blood. But Yaqub knew, deep in his heart, that his own sons were lying to his face. And he turned to the only thing that could hold him. Beautiful patience." *(Qur'an 12:16-18, shown not recited)*

7. **[SHOT: a caravan of camels crossing dunes at golden dusk]**
   "Days later, a passing caravan stopped at that well to draw water. And when they pulled up the bucket, they found a boy. They took him to Egypt, and there they sold him, for a few cheap coins, as if he were worth nothing at all." *(Qur'an 12:19-20)*

8. **[SHOT: the animated Canaan to Egypt map]**
   "A prophet's son, of the line of Ibrahim, carried hundreds of miles from his father, and sold in the markets of a foreign land." *(Qur'an 12:21)*

## Act II — Egypt, the test, the prison

9. **[SHOT: towering ancient Egyptian columns in warm light]**
   "But Allah does not abandon His own. Yusuf was bought by one of the most powerful men in Egypt, who told his wife, take good care of him. And in that grand house, the boy grew into a young man." *(Qur'an 12:21-22)*

10. **[SHOT: a sweep of radiant light across a dark sky]**
    "And he grew into no ordinary man. On the night the Prophet, peace be upon him, was raised up through the heavens, he met Yusuf. And he told us that Yusuf had been given half of all the beauty in creation." *(Sahih Muslim 162)*

11. **[SHOT: a long corridor in a great house, doors closing one after another]**
    "In that house lived the minister's wife. And as the years passed, she fell for the young man under her own roof. Until one day, she sent every servant away, and closed the doors of the house, one after another, until there was nowhere left for him to go. And she turned to him." *(Qur'an 12:23)*

12. **[SHOT: a hand drawing back, turning toward a window of light]**
    "And Yusuf refused her. I seek refuge in Allah, he said. Your husband has trusted me, and treated me well. How could I ever wrong him? How could I disobey my Lord?" *(Qur'an 12:23)*

13. **[SHOT: the hem of a robe tearing as someone runs]**
    "He turned and ran for the door. She caught him, and his shirt tore in her hands. And just as the door flew open, her husband was standing on the other side." *(Qur'an 12:25)*

14. **[SHOT: women seated at a feast, fruit and small knives in their hands]**
    "Word of it spread through the city. The women whispered that the great minister's wife had fallen for a servant boy. So she invited them all to a banquet, and placed a knife and a piece of fruit in every hand. And then she called Yusuf to walk into the room. The moment they laid eyes on him, they were so overwhelmed that they cut their own hands, and never even felt the blade." *(Qur'an 12:31)*

15. **[SHOT: a heavy iron-barred door swinging shut]**
    "Now you see, she told them. Then she turned back to Yusuf, with a threat. Obey me, or you will be thrown into prison. And Yusuf made his choice. My Lord, he prayed, prison is dearer to me than the sin they are calling me to. And so, though he had done nothing wrong, the doors of a prison closed behind him." *(Qur'an 12:32-35)*

16. **[SHOT: two young prisoners sitting in the gloom of a cell]**
    "But even there, Yusuf was still Yusuf. Two young men were thrown in beside him, each carrying a strange dream he could not understand. They came to him for answers. And before he said a single word about their dreams, he asked them something far more important." *(Qur'an 12:36-37)*

17. **[SHOT: a single shaft of light through a barred window]** + on-screen ayah
    "O my two companions of prison. Are many scattered lords better, or Allah, the One, the Supreme?" *(Qur'an 12:39, shown not recited)*

18. **[SHOT: dark wine grapes ripening on the vine, close up (no wine, no goblet)]**
    "Then he told them what their dreams meant. One of you will be set free, he said, and will pour wine for the king once more. The other will be put to death. And to the one who would live, Yusuf said softly, remember me to your master." *(Qur'an 12:41-42)*

19. **[SHOT: an empty cell, a slow shaft of light crossing the floor]**
    "But when that man walked free, he forgot all about Yusuf. And so Yusuf stayed in that prison, forgotten by everyone, for several more years." *(Qur'an 12:42)*

## Act III — The rise, the famine, the reunion

20. **[SHOT: cattle grazing at dawn, then green and withered ears of wheat]**
    "Until one night, the king of Egypt woke from a dream that shook him. Seven fat cows, swallowed whole by seven starving ones. Seven green ears of grain, and seven dry. No one in his court could explain it." *(Qur'an 12:43)*

21. **[SHOT: endless golden wheat, then full sacks in a granary]**
    "And at last, the freed servant remembered the young man in the prison. They brought the dream to Yusuf, and he read it clearly. Seven years of plenty are coming, he said, and after them, seven years of famine. Plant all you can, and store it. It will keep your people alive." *(Qur'an 12:46-49)*

22. **[SHOT: a prison door opening onto blinding daylight]**
    "Amazed, the king ordered him set free at once. But Yusuf would not simply walk out. First, he said, go back and ask about the women who cut their hands. Let the truth be known. He would leave that cell with his name cleared, or he would not leave at all." *(Qur'an 12:50-51)*

23. **[SHOT: sunlight pouring through breaking clouds]**
    "It is a patience almost beyond us. The Prophet, peace be upon him, once said that had he been kept in prison as long as Yusuf, and then the call to freedom came, he would have hurried straight out. But Yusuf waited, for his honour." *(Sahih al-Bukhari 3372)*

24. **[SHOT: vast granaries, mountains of grain]**
    "His innocence was proven before the whole court. And the boy who had been thrown into a well, sold, and forgotten in a cell, was placed in charge of the storehouses of all of Egypt." *(Qur'an 12:54-55)*

25. **[SHOT: cracked, lifeless earth under a hard sun]**
    "Then, just as he had foretold, the famine came. And it spread far beyond Egypt, until it reached the hills of Canaan, and a grieving old father, and ten desperate brothers." *(Qur'an 12:58)*

26. **[SHOT: travel-worn men bowing low before a seated figure]**
    "They came down to Egypt to beg for grain. And they bowed before the most powerful man in the land, never once realizing that they were looking into the eyes of the little brother they had buried in a well." *(Qur'an 12:58)*

27. **[SHOT: a golden cup slipped quietly into a sack of grain]**
    "Yusuf knew them at once. He tested them. And to keep his youngest brother safely at his side, he had the king's own cup hidden in the boy's bag. It was a plan, the Qur'an tells us, that Allah Himself taught him." *(Qur'an 12:70, 12:76)*

28. **[SHOT: an old man's clouded eyes, his trembling hands rising]** + on-screen ayah
    "And far away in Canaan, Yaqub was still grieving. He had wept for Yusuf for so many years that his eyesight had faded to white. And still, he would not give up hope. I complain, he said, only of my grief and my sorrow, to Allah." *(Qur'an 12:84, 12:86 shown not recited)*

29. **[SHOT: a circle of brothers frozen, faces falling]**
    "At last, when the brothers stood before him one final time, broken and desperate, Yusuf could hold it back no longer. I am Yusuf, he said. And this is my brother. They froze. And they knew, in that instant, exactly what they had done." *(Qur'an 12:89-90)*

30. **[SHOT: a single open hand extended palm-up toward soft light]** + on-screen ayah
    "They were completely in his power. He could have done anything to them. Instead, he said, there is no blame upon you today. May Allah forgive you. He is the most merciful of the merciful." *(Qur'an 12:92, shown not recited)*

31. **[SHOT: the gates of a city, a vast crowd gathered in the sun]**
    "And centuries later, another man would stand exactly where Yusuf stood. When the Prophet, peace be upon him, entered Makkah, over the very people who had driven him out and killed his companions, he asked them what they thought he would do. Then he forgave them, with almost the same words. Go, he said. You are free." *(Conquest of Makkah, seerah)*

32. **[SHOT: a white shirt lifting on the wind across open land, then closed eyes opening to light]**
    "Yusuf sent his shirt home to his father. And before it had even arrived, the old man lifted his head and said, I can sense the scent of Yusuf, though you will think I am a foolish old man. They laid the shirt over his face. And his sight came rushing back." *(Qur'an 12:93-96)*

33. **[SHOT: eleven stars, the sun and the moon, rising over a horizon]**
    "Then his family came to him. His parents, and his eleven brothers, bowed before him in honour. And the dream of that little boy, the eleven stars, the sun and the moon, had come true, exactly as he had seen it all those years before." *(Qur'an 12:100)*

34. **[SHOT: hands lifted in dua at first light]** + on-screen ayah
    "My Lord, Yusuf prayed, You have given me power, and taught me. You are my protector in this world, and the next. Let me die in submission to You, and join me with the righteous." *(Qur'an 12:101, shown not recited)*

35. **[SHOT: a single trail of footprints leading toward the sunrise]**
    "He was betrayed by his own blood. Enslaved. Lied about. And left to rot in a prison for years. And every single step of it was quietly carrying him exactly where Allah meant for him to be. So whatever you are walking through tonight, do not lose hope. Trust the One who is writing your story." *(reflection)*
