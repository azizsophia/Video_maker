# Week batch - 14 scripts for one-sitting review

7 shorts (vertical, Khadijah cadence: hook, gold title card read deep, sourced
beats, reflective close) and 7 long-forms (16:9, ~9 min, chaptered, same title
card treatment). Every claim carries its on-screen source in [brackets]. Ayat
are SHOWN via the Quran.com pipeline, never recited. No emojis, hyphens only.

HOW TO REVIEW FAST: read each HOOK, the TITLE line, skim the beats, read the
CLOSE. Flag anything by day + beat number (e.g. "Tue short beat 5"). Names to
ear-test before render: Dajjal, Ayyub, Salman, Suraqah, Yunus, Sulayman.

## MONDAY

### MON SHORT - The Signs Already Here (~2 min)
Pillar: Signs of the Hour.

1. HOOK [Sahih al-Bukhari 50 and Sahih Muslim 8]
"Fourteen hundred years ago, a man who could not read or write... listed the
signs that would come before the end of the world. Towers. Wealth. Time
speeding up. And you are living inside that list... right now."
Visual: night city skyline timelapse.

2. TITLE CARD - THE SIGNS / of the Final Hour (deep, serious read)
"These are the signs of the Hour... and they are already here."
Visual: slow dark storm clouds.

3. [Sahih Muslim 8]
"One day, a stranger appeared before the Prophet, peace be upon him. Clothes of
pure white... hair jet black... no trace of travel on him. Yet no one in the
city knew him. He sat knee to knee with the Prophet... and began to ask."
Visual: white fabric moving in dark wind.

4. [Sahih Muslim 8]
"He asked about Islam. About faith. And then he asked... when is the Hour? And
the Prophet, peace be upon him, gave the only truthful answer there is. The one
who is asked... knows no more than the one who asks."
Visual: vast night desert sky.

5. [Sahih al-Bukhari 50]
"But I will tell you its signs, he said. The slave woman will give birth to her
own mistress. And you will see barefoot, destitute shepherds of goats...
competing in building the tallest towers."
Visual: skyscrapers shot from below, glass and sky.

6. (reflection, no new claim)
"Look at the skylines rising from the Gulf today... glass towers racing each
other upward... on the very sands where shepherds walked barefoot, within
living memory."
Visual: modern desert-city skyline at dusk.

7. [Sahih al-Bukhari 6037]
"And he told us more. Time itself will feel like it is shrinking. Good deeds
will grow fewer. And killing... will increase."
Visual: fast-moving clouds timelapse over city lights.

8. [Sahih al-Bukhari 1412]
"And wealth will flood the earth... until a man walks out with charity in his
hand, searching for someone who will accept it... and finds no one."
Visual: gold dust drifting in the dark.

9. [Sahih al-Bukhari 100]
"And knowledge will not be ripped out of hearts. It will leave quietly... with
the death of the scholars. Until people take ignorant leaders... who are asked,
and answer without knowledge... misguided, and misguiding."
Visual: a lamp dimming in a dark room.

10. [Sahih Muslim 8]
"Then the stranger rose... and vanished. And the Prophet, peace be upon him,
said... that was Jibril. He came to teach you your religion."
Visual: empty desert horizon, wind over sand.

11. QUOTE - Qur'an 47:18 (shown, not recited)
"And the Qur'an says it plainly... do they wait for anything but the Hour, to
come upon them suddenly? Its signs... have already come."
Visual: dark clouds parting over light.

12. CLOSE (reflection)
"He never gave us a date. Because the signs were never for counting down...
they were for waking up. The question was never, when is the Hour. It is...
where will you be standing, when it finds you."
Visual: sunrise breaking over a still landscape.

### MON LONG - The Dajjal: The Trial Every Prophet Warned About (~9 min)
Pillar: Prophecy. No date-setting, no speculation beyond the texts.

CHAPTER 1 - THE WARNING
1. HOOK [Sahih al-Bukhari 7131] "There is one trial so severe... that every
single prophet, from Nuh onward, warned his own people about it. One man. One
test. And the Prophet Muhammad, peace be upon him, told us more about him than
anyone before."
2. TITLE CARD - THE DAJJAL / the greatest trial (deep read) "This is what the
Prophet, peace be upon him, taught us about the Dajjal... so that we would
never be deceived."

CHAPTER 2 - WHO HE IS
3. [Sahih al-Bukhari 7131] "He will come as a man... young, with crisp curled
hair, and one eye... like a floating grape. And between his eyes, written...
kaf, fa, ra. Disbeliever. And every believer will read it... whether he can
read or not." [Sahih Muslim 2934]
4. [Sahih Muslim 2937] "He will emerge on a road between Sham and Iraq... and
he will move through the earth quickly, like rain driven by wind."

CHAPTER 3 - THE TRIAL
5. [Sahih Muslim 2937] "He will come to a people and call them, and they will
believe him. He will command the sky... and it will rain. The earth... and it
will grow. Their herds will return to them fat, with full udders."
6. [Sahih al-Bukhari 3450] "He will carry with him something like Paradise,
and something like Fire. But his paradise will be fire... and his fire, cool
water. That is the trick at the heart of him... everything reversed."
7. [Sahih al-Bukhari 1882] "In Madinah's outskirts, a believer will face him
and call him a liar. The Dajjal will kill him... then bring him back to life.
And the man will say... by Allah, I have never been more certain of who you
are than I am now."
8. [Sahih Muslim 2937] "He will remain forty days. One day like a year... one
like a month... one like a week. The companions asked, will one day's prayers
be enough for the day like a year? He said no... estimate its measure."

CHAPTER 4 - WHERE HE CANNOT GO
9. [Sahih al-Bukhari 1881] "But two cities he will never enter... Makkah and
Madinah. At every gate, angels standing guard."

CHAPTER 5 - THE END OF HIM
10. [Sahih Muslim 2937] "Then Isa, son of Maryam, alayhi salam, will descend
at the white minaret east of Damascus... and the Dajjal will melt before him
like salt melting in water... and it will end at the gate of Ludd."

CHAPTER 6 - YOUR PROTECTION (the practical close)
11. [Sahih Muslim 809] "So how do you protect yourself from the greatest trial
ever to walk the earth? The Prophet, peace be upon him, left us the keys.
Memorize the first ten verses of Surah al-Kahf."
12. [Sahih Muslim 588] "And in every single prayer, before the salam... seek
refuge in Allah from four things. The punishment of the grave, the punishment
of the Fire, the trial of life and death... and the trial of the Dajjal."
13. [Sunan Abi Dawud 4319, sahih] "And if you hear of him... do not go to look.
Keep far from him. For a man will come thinking himself firm in faith... and
follow him, because of the doubts he raises."
14. CLOSE [Sahih Muslim 2937] "The Prophet, peace be upon him, said... if he
comes while I am among you, I will contend with him for you. And if he comes
after me... then each of you must contend for himself, and Allah is my
successor over every Muslim. He told us everything... not to frighten us. To
arm us."

## TUESDAY

### TUE SHORT - The Creature That Receives Revelation (~90s)
Pillar: Qur'an and creation. Framed as awe, zero science overreach.

1. HOOK [Qur'an 16:68]
"There is one creature on this earth that receives direct inspiration from
Allah. It is not an angel. It is not a prophet. It fits... on your fingertip."
Visual: macro flower field in golden light (no insects with faces needed;
a bee silhouette on lavender is ideal).

2. TITLE CARD - AN-NAHL / the bee (deep read)
"This is the bee... the creature Allah named a surah after."
Visual: slow dark honey glow.

3. QUOTE - Qur'an 16:68 (shown)
"And your Lord inspired the bee... take for yourself homes in the mountains,
and in the trees, and in what they build."
Visual: mountain wildflower meadow.

4. [Qur'an 16:69]
"Then eat from all the fruits... and follow the paths of your Lord, made easy
for you. That is her entire life. Obedience... on a path her Lord already
smoothed."
Visual: bee flying between blossoms, soft focus.

5. QUOTE - Qur'an 16:69 (shown)
"From their bellies comes a drink of varying colors... in which there is
healing for people."
Visual: honey pouring, backlit amber.

6. [Sahih al-Bukhari 5684]
"A man came to the Prophet, peace be upon him, and said... my brother's stomach
is ill. He said, give him honey. Then again. And again. Until he said... Allah
has spoken the truth, and your brother's stomach has lied. And the man was
cured."
Visual: warm honey in glass jar, dark background.

7. (reflection)
"She never eats except what is pure. She never follows except the path made
for her. And what comes out of her... heals other people."
Visual: sunlit honeycomb texture.

8. CLOSE (reflection)
"An entire chapter of the Qur'an carries her name. Not the lion. Not the
eagle. The bee. Perhaps because Allah is telling us... a small life, lived in
obedience, that leaves healing behind it... is a great life."
Visual: golden field at sunset, wind moving through it.

### TUE LONG - Ayyub: The Most Patient Man Who Ever Lived (~9 min)
Pillar: Prophet stories. Rides the sabr theme. No Israiliyyat (no wife's name,
no invented dialogue, duration kept unspecified).

CHAPTER 1 - THE MAN ALLAH PRAISED
1. HOOK [Qur'an 38:44] "In the entire Qur'an, only a handful of people are
praised by Allah with the words... what an excellent servant. One of them was
a man who lost everything. And the reason Allah praised him... is what he did
next."
2. TITLE CARD - AYYUB / alayhi salam (deep read) "This is the story of
Ayyub... alayhi salam."
3. [Seerah] "He was a prophet of abundance. Wealth. Land. Cattle. A large
family. And a heart that never let the blessings make him forget the Giver."

CHAPTER 2 - THE TEST
4. [Seerah] "Then the tests came... in waves. His wealth went. His children
were taken. And then the illness came for his own body... until the people
who once sat at his table... withdrew from him."
5. [Qur'an 21:83] "Years upon years passed. And through all of it, one person
never left... his wife. She stayed, she worked, she carried him. And still,
Ayyub did not curse his fate. He did not demand. He did not despair."

CHAPTER 3 - THE DUA
6. QUOTE - Qur'an 21:83 (shown) "Listen to how a prophet complains. He turned
to his Lord and said only this... indeed, adversity has touched me... and You
are the Most Merciful of the merciful. That is the whole dua. He states his
pain... he praises his Lord... and he does not even name a request. He leaves
the answer to Allah."
7. (reflection) "No accusation. No how could You. Not even, cure me. Just...
You see me, and You are Mercy itself. Fourteen centuries later, we are still
learning what that sentence knows."

CHAPTER 4 - THE ANSWER
8. QUOTE - Qur'an 38:42 (shown) "And the answer came. Strike the ground with
your foot... here is cool water, to wash with, and to drink. The cure had
been under his feet all along... waiting for the moment Allah willed."
9. QUOTE - Qur'an 21:84 (shown) "So We responded to him, and removed what
afflicted him. And We gave him back his family... and the like of them with
them... a mercy from Us, and a reminder for the worshippers."
10. [Sahih al-Bukhari 279] "And one day, while Ayyub bathed, golden locusts
began to fall on him... and he rushed to gather them in his garment. His Lord
called to him... O Ayyub, did I not make you rich enough? He said... yes, my
Lord. But I can never have enough... of Your blessing."

CHAPTER 5 - THE GENTLENESS AT THE END
11. [Qur'an 38:44] "During the illness he had sworn an oath to discipline his
wife, a hundred strikes... and now he regretted it, for she had been nothing
but faithful. So Allah Himself showed him the way out... take a bundle of
grass in your hand, and touch her with it once, and your oath is kept. The
Lord who tested him... protected her, and softened the law for him."
12. CLOSE (reflection) "Sabr does not mean feeling no pain. Ayyub felt all of
it. Sabr means complaining TO Allah... instead of complaining ABOUT Allah.
That is why, forever, Allah calls him... what an excellent servant, always
turning back to his Lord."

### WED SHORT - The Three Verses That Answered a Taunt (~90s)
Pillar: Surah decoded. Surah al-Kawthar.

1. HOOK [Tafsir of Surah 108]
"The shortest surah in the Qur'an is three verses long. You can say it in ten
seconds. And it came down from heaven... to defend a grieving father."
Visual: dark still desert night.

2. TITLE CARD - AL-KAWTHAR / the abundance (deep read)
"This is Surah al-Kawthar... the shortest surah in the Qur'an."
Visual: slow gold dust in black.

3. [Seerah]
"The Prophet, peace be upon him, buried his infant sons... Qasim, and later
Abdullah. He was a father lowering his own children into the earth."
Visual: single dim lantern in darkness.

4. [Tafsir of Surah 108]
"And the men of Quraysh saw his grief... and mocked it. He is abtar, they
said. Cut off. No sons, no line, no future. When he dies... his name dies
with him."
Visual: wind stripping sand off a dune crest.

5. QUOTE - Qur'an 108:1 (shown)
"And Allah answered from above seven heavens... Indeed, We have granted you
al-Kawthar. The abundance. Not one son. Not one legacy. Abundance itself."
Visual: light flooding through clouds.

6. [Sahih Muslim 400]
"The Prophet, peace be upon him, later described it... a river in Paradise,
promised to him by his Lord... upon it, abundant good."
Visual: pristine flowing water catching light.

7. QUOTE - Qur'an 108:3 (shown)
"And the surah ends with the reversal... indeed, your enemy is the one cut
off. Not you. Him."
Visual: a dead branch in dark wind.

8. CLOSE (reflection)
"Today, no one names their sons after the men who mocked him. And the man
they called cut off... is honored by name, billions of times a day, in every
prayer on earth. When people mock what you lost... remember the One who
counts what you have been given."
Visual: crowds of lights / Madinah skyline glow at night (no faces).

### WED LONG - Salman al-Farisi: The Search Across Empires (~9 min)
Pillar: Companions deep. Source: Salman's own narrative, Musnad Ahmad (sahih
chain); seerah where noted. The weak "Salman is from us" line is NOT used.

CHAPTER 1 - THE FIRE KEEPER'S SON
1. HOOK [Musnad Ahmad] "He was born worshipping fire in Persia... and he died
a companion of the Prophet, peace be upon him. Between those two sentences lie
two empires, five teachers, chains, slavery... and the longest search for
truth ever told. And we have it in his own words."
2. TITLE CARD - SALMAN AL-FARISI / may Allah be pleased with him (deep read)
3. [Musnad Ahmad] "His father was a Persian chief who loved him so much he
kept him home... like a girl kept behind curtains. And he made him keeper of
the sacred fire... the flame that must never go out."

CHAPTER 2 - THE FIRST CRACK
4. [Musnad Ahmad] "Sent one day to the family estate, he passed a church...
and heard men praying. He walked in. And he stayed until sunset. This, he
said... is better than the religion we are upon."
5. [Musnad Ahmad] "His father's answer was iron on his ankles... a chain, and
a locked house. But you cannot chain a question. Salman sent word to the
Christians... when a caravan leaves for Sham, tell me. And he broke free, and
went with it."

CHAPTER 3 - MONK TO MONK
6. [Musnad Ahmad] "In Sham he served the bishop of the church... and found him
corrupt. A man who commanded charity, then hoarded it in jars of gold. When he
died, Salman showed the people his hidden jars of gold and silver... and
they denied him an honored burial."
7. [Musnad Ahmad] "Then came a righteous teacher... and when he was dying,
Salman asked, to whom do you send me? Man by man... Mosul... Nasibin...
Ammuriyyah... each teacher dying, each pointing onward. Decades of his life,
handed from one dying light to the next."
8. [Musnad Ahmad] "And the last one told him... the time of a prophet is near.
He will appear in a land of date palms, between two tracts of black stone. He
accepts a gift, but will not eat charity. And between his shoulders... is the
seal of prophethood."

CHAPTER 4 - SOLD
9. [Musnad Ahmad] "Salman paid a caravan of merchants to carry him toward that
land. And in the middle of the desert... they betrayed him. They sold him as a
slave. The seeker of truth... traded like cattle, carried off to serve, until
he was sold again... to a man from Yathrib. And when Salman saw the date
palms... he knew."

CHAPTER 5 - THE THREE SIGNS
10. [Musnad Ahmad] "Then one day, working atop a palm tree, he heard it... a
man has arrived in Quba, claiming to be a prophet. Salman says... I began to
tremble so hard I nearly fell on my master below."
11. [Musnad Ahmad] "He tested him. He brought food and said, this is charity...
and the Prophet, peace be upon him, told his companions eat, and did not touch
it. Sign one. He brought food and said, this is a gift... and he ate. Sign
two. Then at a funeral, Salman circled behind him... and the Prophet, knowing,
let his cloak fall from his back. And there it was... the seal. And Salman
fell upon it, kissing it, weeping."

CHAPTER 6 - FREE
12. [Musnad Ahmad] "His freedom was bought the way everything in his life was
earned... slowly, with his hands. Three hundred date palms, planted one by
one... and the Prophet, peace be upon him, planted them with his own hands.
Every single tree took root."
13. [Seerah] "Years later, when ten thousand enemies marched on Madinah, it
was Salman who said... in Persia, we would dig a trench. And the trench saved
the city."
14. CLOSE (reflection) "He crossed two empires, five teachers, chains, and
slavery... across decades... for one truth. Most of us were handed that truth
at birth... and some of us have never once opened it. Ask yourself what
Salman would have given... for what is already in your hands."

## THURSDAY

### THU SHORT - Sumayya: The First Martyr (~2 min) - FINAL, fixes applied
Pillar: Companions. (Merged torture beats; close split so the button lands on
her alone. Mostly seerah sources, labeled honestly; the Paradise narration is
graded sahih by al-Hakim with adh-Dhahabi concurring.)

1. HOOK [Seerah (Ibn Sa'd)]
"The first person to ever die for Islam... was not a soldier. It was an old
woman. With no tribe, no wealth, and no one to protect her. And her name has
been honored for fourteen hundred years."
Visual: lone dead tree in desert dusk.

2. TITLE CARD - SUMAYYA / may Allah be pleased with her (deep read)
"This is the story of Sumayya... may Allah be pleased with her."
Visual: slow dark starfield.

3. [Seerah (Ibn Ishaq)]
"In the earliest days of Makkah, when the believers could be counted on two
hands... she was one of them. A freed slave. Among the weakest, most
powerless people in the whole city."
Visual: narrow dark alley of old stone.

4. [Seerah (Ibn Hisham)]
"With her husband Yasir... and her son Ammar... she believed in one God. And
in a city that bowed to hundreds of idols, for people with no protection...
that belief was a death sentence."
Visual: three small flames in darkness.

5. [Seerah (Ibn Ishaq)]
"So Quraysh dragged the family of Yasir into the burning desert sun. Day
after day, they laid them on the scorching sand... to force them to abandon
their Lord. And they would not."
Visual: heat shimmer over blinding sand.

6. [al-Hakim, sahih]
"And the Prophet, peace be upon him, would pass them as they suffered...
unable to stop it. And he said to them... Patience, O family of Yasir.
Patience. For your appointed place... is Paradise."
Visual: single shaft of light through dust.

7. [Seerah (Ibn Sa'd)]
"Abu Jahl himself came to break her. He mocked her. He beat her. But this old
woman would not bow to him... or to his idols. She held on to her God... to
her very last breath."
Visual: rock steadfast against crashing wind or sand.

8. [Seerah (Ibn Sa'd)]
"So in his rage... he killed her. And Sumayya, an elderly woman who owned
nothing in this world... became the first soul ever to die for Islam. The
first martyr... of this entire religion."
Visual: a flame going out, ember smoke rising.

9. [Tafsir of Qur'an 16:106]
"Her son Ammar was tortured so brutally... they forced words of disbelief
from his mouth. He came to the Prophet, shaking and weeping. And Allah
Himself... sent down his defense."
Visual: storm clouds breaking.

10. QUOTE - Qur'an 16:106 (shown)
"Except the one who is forced... while his heart is at peace... with faith."
Visual: calm light after rain.

11. [Sunan an-Nasa'i 5010]
"Her son lived on to become one of the most beloved of companions. And of
him, the Prophet, peace be upon him, said... Ammar is filled with faith, to
the very marrow of his bones."
Visual: strong dawn light over mountains.

12. CLOSE (reflection)
"She had no army... no wealth... no name Makkah respected. But she gave the
one thing she owned. And Allah made her the first, forever. That was
Sumayya... may Allah be pleased with her."
Visual: single star brightening in deep night sky.

### THU LONG - The Hijra: The Greatest Escape in History (~9 min)
Pillar: Islamic history. Includes the honesty beat: the spider web story is
NOT in the authentic accounts, and we say so.

CHAPTER 1 - THE PLOT
1. HOOK [Sahih al-Bukhari 3905] "A bounty of one hundred camels... a fortune...
placed on the heads of two unarmed men. Every tracker in Arabia hunting them.
And their hiding place... a hole in a rock, three days, with the hunters
standing close enough to touch."
2. TITLE CARD - THE HIJRA / the migration (deep read)
3. QUOTE - Qur'an 8:30 (shown) "Makkah had decided... one young man from every
clan, one strike together, so the blood would be on every tribe. And the
Qur'an records it... and when those who disbelieved plotted against you, to
confine you, or kill you, or expel you. They plan... and Allah plans. And
Allah is the best of planners."
4. [Seerah] "That night, Ali, may Allah be pleased with him, lay down in the
Prophet's bed, wrapped in his cloak... a young man volunteering to absorb the
swords meant for someone else. And the Prophet, peace be upon him, walked out
past the assassins... unseen."

CHAPTER 2 - THE COMPANION
5. [Sahih al-Bukhari 3905] "Aisha, may Allah be pleased with her, tells it...
the Prophet came to our house at noon, an hour he never came. And when he told
Abu Bakr, you are my companion on the journey... my father wept. I had never
known, she said, that a person could weep... from joy."
6. [Sahih al-Bukhari 3905] "The plan was exact. Two camels, prepared. Asma
binte Abi Bakr carried food in secret... Abdullah ibn Abi Bakr slept among
Quraysh and brought their plans each night... and Amir ibn Fuhayrah grazed
sheep over the footprints, wiping the trail clean."

CHAPTER 3 - THE CAVE
7. [Sahih al-Bukhari 3653] "For three nights they hid in the cave of Thawr.
And the trackers came... all the way to the mouth of the cave. Abu Bakr
whispered... if one of them looks down at his feet, he will see us. And the
Prophet, peace be upon him, answered with one of the greatest sentences ever
spoken... O Abu Bakr, what do you think of two... whose third is Allah?"
8. QUOTE - Qur'an 9:40 (shown) "And Allah sealed that moment into the Qur'an
forever... when the two were in the cave, when he said to his companion, do
not grieve... indeed Allah is with us."
9. HONESTY BEAT (no source, deliberately) "You may have heard that a spider
spun a web across the cave mouth, and a dove nested there. Here is the truth...
that detail does not appear in the authentic narrations. And honestly, the
authentic account is greater. Because it was not a web that blinded the
trackers... it was Allah."

CHAPTER 4 - THE CHASE
10. [Sahih al-Bukhari 3906] "They rode north by the coast road, and the bounty
did its work. A horseman named Suraqah spotted them... the hundred camels
within his reach. Twice... three times... his horse sank to its knees in firm
ground. And Suraqah understood he was chasing something protected. The hunter
turned back... and turned the other hunters away. By nightfall, the man who
came for the bounty... was guarding the man he hunted."

CHAPTER 5 - ARRIVAL
11. [Sahih al-Bukhari 3906] "And then, Madinah. The city had been climbing to
the rooftops every morning, watching the horizon. And when he arrived, peace
be upon him, the city rang with takbir... Allahu akbar, the Messenger of Allah has
come. The city that
would carry his name forever. Madinat an-Nabi. The city of the Prophet."
12. CLOSE (reflection) "Everything about the Hijra was planned to perfection...
the decoy, the supplies, the spy, the tracks, the route. And still, in the
end, it came down to a moment where planning could do nothing at all... and
trust did everything. Tie your camel, plan your escape... and when the
hunters reach the mouth of your cave, say what he said... do not grieve.
Indeed, Allah is with us."

## FRIDAY

### FRI SHORT - The Dua From the Belly of the Whale (~90s)
Pillar: Prophets and Seerah. Yunus alayhi salam.

1. HOOK [Jami at-Tirmidhi 3505, sahih]
"From inside a living whale... at the bottom of the sea... in the black of
night... one sentence saved a prophet. And the Prophet Muhammad, peace be
upon him, said... no Muslim ever says it, for anything, except that Allah
answers him."
Visual: deep dark ocean water, faint light from above.

2. TITLE CARD - YUNUS / alayhi salam (deep read)
"This is the story of Yunus... alayhi salam."
Visual: slow moonlit sea.

3. [Qur'an 21:87]
"He had called his city to Allah for years... and they refused. So he left
them, angry... before his Lord gave him leave. And he thought no decree
would tighten around him."
Visual: storm gathering over sea horizon.

4. [Qur'an 37:140-141]
"He boarded a loaded ship. The storm came. And the sailors cast lots to
lighten the load... and the lot fell... on him."
Visual: violent waves in darkness.

5. [Qur'an 37:142]
"And the whale swallowed him. A living prison, inside the sea, inside the
night. Darkness... upon darkness... upon darkness."
Visual: descending into deep black water.

6. QUOTE - Qur'an 21:87 (shown)
"And from inside all three darknesses, he called... There is no god but You.
Glory be to You. Indeed, I was of the wrongdoers. No excuses. Praise...
then honesty."
Visual: a single beam of light piercing deep water.

7. QUOTE - Qur'an 21:88 (shown)
"And the answer is recorded forever... So We responded to him, and saved him
from the distress. And listen to the ending... AND THUS do We save the
believers. That promise is not his. It is ours."
Visual: sunlight breaking the surface from below.

8. [Jami at-Tirmidhi 3505]
"The Prophet, peace be upon him, said... the dua of my brother Yunus. No
Muslim says it, in any difficulty, except that Allah answers him."
Visual: calm sea at dawn.

9. CLOSE (reflection)
"Whatever you are inside right now... it is not three darknesses at the
bottom of the sea. Say the words. La ilaha illa anta... subhanaka... inni
kuntu minaz-zalimin..."
